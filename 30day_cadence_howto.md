# 1. 30-Day Content Cadence (Low-Noise, High-Signal)

This cadence is designed so that:

* you post **2–3 times per week**
* every post reinforces the same story
* speculation never becomes the center
* you don’t burn out

Think of this as **rhythm, not volume**.

---

## Week 0 (Pre-Launch Seeding – Private)

**Goal:** Warm up the right people quietly.

**Actions (no public posting yet):**

* Share `why.md` + `charter.md` privately
* Ask for *feel*, not feedback
* Listen more than you talk

No public countdowns.

---

## Week 1 (Launch Week – Signal)

**Goal:** Introduce tokenX clearly and calmly.

### Day 1 — Launch Thread (Public)

* Post the full thread we wrote
* Pin the Charter + FAQ
* Do **nothing else** that day

### Day 3 — Clarifying Post

> “A reminder: tokenX is not an investment pitch. It’s an open experiment in participation. Reading > reacting.”

Link to docs.

### Day 5 — Builder Call

> “If you’re a builder curious about tokenX, I’m hosting a small, informal call next week. No pitch. Just discussion.”

Collect emails via a simple form (Notion/Google Form).

---

## Week 2 (Context & Education)

**Goal:** Replace confusion with understanding.

### Post 1 — “What tokenX is NOT”

Short list. Calm tone. Link FAQ.

### Post 2 — “Why culture matters here”

Tie Caribbean culture → coordination → crypto.
No token mention until the end.

### Optional: Share the explainer article

Position it as education, not marketing.

---

## Week 3 (Participation & Proof)

**Goal:** Show real activity, however small.

### Post 1 — “Something happened”

> “A small group met / built / discussed tokenX this week. No announcements. Just participation.”

Photos or screenshots help.

### Post 2 — “What surprised me”

Founder reflection post:

* what you expected
* what you didn’t
* what you’re still unsure about

Vulnerability builds trust.

---

## Week 4 (Momentum Without Hype)

**Goal:** Encourage action without coercion.

### Post 1 — “If you’re here, do something”

List examples:

* write
* build
* host
* critique
* remix

### Post 2 — “What’s next (loosely)”

No roadmap.
Just themes:

* experimentation
* culture
* builders

End with:

> “Nothing here is mandatory.”

---

## Posting Frequency Summary

* 2 posts/week average
* 1 thread total
* 1–2 reflective posts
* 1 invitation
* 1 participation prompt

That’s enough.

# 2. Who to Seed First (Very Specific)

You do **not** seed “crypto people” broadly.

You seed **connectors**.

### Priority Tier 1 (10–15 people)

These are:

* Caribbean or diaspora builders
* respected, not loud
* already shipping things
* not influencer-brained

Examples:

* Caribbean startup founders
* open-source maintainers from the region
* AI / crypto engineers with roots in the Caribbean
* thoughtful writers with small but engaged audiences

**Why:**
If *they* understand tokenX, others will follow naturally.

---

### Priority Tier 2 (10–20 people)

These are:

* artists
* musicians
* cultural organizers
* event hosts
* community leaders

They don’t care about tokenomics — and that’s good.

**Why:**
They bring *expression*, not speculation.

---

### Priority Tier 3 (Optional, later)

* crypto researchers
* protocol builders
* thoughtful VCs (not degens)
* ecosystem leads (Sui, ETH L2s)

Only after the narrative is stable.

---

## Who to Avoid Seeding Early

* traders
* influencers
* “early alpha” hunters
* anonymous hype accounts
* anyone who asks “what’s the upside?” first

They will distort the signal.

# 3. Exact Outreach Messages (Copy/Paste Ready)

### A. Personal DM (Builder / Founder)

> Hey — I’m working on something experimental and would value your *intuition*, not promotion.
>
> It’s called tokenX. It’s a Caribbean-rooted participation experiment using crypto — no promises, no pitch.
>
> If you have 10 minutes, I’d love your honest reaction to this page: [why.md link].
>
> Totally fine if it’s not your thing.

Why this works:

* low pressure
* respectful
* filters hype-seekers

---

### B. Artist / Cultural Organizer DM

> Hi — I’m exploring an open crypto experiment rooted in Caribbean culture and coordination.
>
> It’s not a product or investment — more a shared primitive people can build and express with.
>
> I’d love to know if this resonates at all from a cultural perspective: [why.md link].
>
> No expectations — just curiosity.

Why this works:

* avoids jargon
* invites interpretation
* doesn’t tokenize them

---

### C. Email (Longer, Thoughtful)

Subject: *An experiment in participation (no pitch)*

> Hi [Name],
>
> I’m reaching out quietly to a small group of people whose thinking I respect.
>
> I’m opening an experiment called tokenX — a Caribbean-rooted crypto network focused on participation, culture, and coordination.
>
> There’s no launch hype, no investment framing, and no expectations. Just an open question I’m exploring publicly.
>
> If you’re curious, here’s the short “why” page: [link].
>
> If not, no worries at all — thanks for reading.

---

### D. Follow-Up (If They Don’t Respond)

Send **once**, after ~10 days.

> Quick follow-up — no pressure at all. Just wanted to share in case it got buried.
>
> Either way, hope you’re well.

That’s it. Don’t chase.
