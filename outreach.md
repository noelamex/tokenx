## The Core Principle (Read This First)

> **You don’t “announce” tokenX.
> You let the right people discover it early — then let them talk.**

If *you* are doing all the talking, it won’t stick.

---

## Phase 0 — Quiet Seeding (2–4 weeks before launch)

**Objective:** Put tokenX in front of *builders, culture carriers, and connectors* — not traders.

### 1. Your Personal Voice (Most Important)

Before any “official” account does anything:

* Post **from your personal account**
* Talk about:

  * the problem
  * the experiment
  * why you’re curious
* Not the token mechanics

Example post:

> “I’ve been thinking a lot about coordination across the Caribbean — culture, diaspora, crypto. We’re opening an experiment soon. No promises, just curiosity.”

This primes people **without triggering speculation**.

---

### 2. Soft-Share the Docs (Selective)

Privately share:

* the **Why page**
* the **Charter**
* the **Tokenomics one-pager**

With:

* Caribbean builders
* diaspora founders
* artists
* thoughtful crypto people
* 10–30 people max

Ask **one question only**:

> “Does this feel honest?”

Not:

* “Would you invest?”
* “Do you think this pumps?”

---

### 3. Create a “Gravity Point”

Before launch, you should have **one stable place**:

* Website (microsite you built)
* GitHub repo
* Or a Notion page

Everything points **there**.
No Telegram shilling yet.

---

## Phase 1 — Signal, Not Blast (Launch Week)

**Objective:** Be visible without being loud.

### 4. One Clean Public Thread

Post the **launch thread** we wrote.

That’s it.

* No countdown
* No “big news”
* No influencer tagging

Let the *content* do the work.

---

### 5. Pin the Guardrails

Immediately pin:

* the **Charter**
* the **FAQ**
* the disclaimer

This does two things:

* filters bad actors
* gives journalists / smart people something to read

---

### 6. Let Others Ask Questions Publicly

When people reply:

* answer clearly
* link docs
* don’t debate price

People watching replies matter more than the repliers.

---

## Phase 2 — Cultural Momentum (Weeks 1–4)

**Objective:** Replace price talk with participation.

### 7. Do One Small, Real Thing

Examples:

* a Caribbean crypto meetup
* a builder call
* a music + tech space
* a simple NFT art drop tied to the idea (not value)

Announce *after* it happens, not before.

> “This happened. It was small. It was real.”

That builds credibility.

---

### 8. Encourage “Do Something” Posts

Prompt the community with:

* “If you’re here, build something.”
* “If you’re here, create something.”
* “If you’re here, host something.”

Participation > promotion.

---

## Phase 3 — Strategic Amplification (Optional)

Only after:

* people are using it
* docs are being read
* culture is forming

Then you can:

### 9. Talk to the Right Media

Not “crypto news.”

Instead:

* culture + tech writers
* diaspora-focused outlets
* thoughtful crypto podcasts
* builders, not hype channels

Your pitch:

> “This is an open experiment. We’re not selling anything.”

That alone is interesting.

---

### 10. Exchanges / Platforms (Later)

When platforms ask:

* you send them the **Charter**
* the **Tokenomics**
* the **FAQ**
* the **Why page**

If they want hype copy, you walk.

That discipline compounds.

---

## What NOT to Do (Very Important)

Do **not**:

* buy engagement
* do influencer deals
* promise early access
* run giveaways tied to price
* open Telegram before you’re ready to moderate
* argue with speculators

Those paths are irreversible.

---

## The Metric That Actually Matters

Not:

* follower count
* price
* impressions

But:

* Are *builders* DMing you?
* Are people asking thoughtful questions?
* Are others explaining tokenX correctly without you?
* Are people doing things without asking permission?

That’s signal.

---

## A Final Reality Check

If tokenX fails quietly:

* you learned
* you protected your integrity
* you didn’t burn credibility

If tokenX succeeds:

* it will be because **others carried it**
* not because you shouted louder

That’s how durable networks begin.
